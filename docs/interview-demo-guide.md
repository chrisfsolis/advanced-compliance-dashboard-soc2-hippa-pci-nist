# Interview Demo Guide: Security Program Scaffolding

## Demo goal

This guided UI presents a synthetic security-program scaffolding dashboard for a small AI company. It is designed to show how an internal security program can define owners, prioritize review queues, automate low-friction evidence collection, and keep human review in the loop for policy, privacy, vendor risk, identity, endpoint operations, BCDR, incident response, finance-partnered controls, and AI tooling.

## 5-minute path

1. Start with **Security Program Scaffolding Demo** and the six summary cards.
2. Review **First Six Months: Build the Scaffolding** to show phased ownership and evidence priorities.
3. Open **Identity & Access Automation** to discuss SSO, MFA, privileged access, SCIM, stale accounts, service accounts, and joiner/mover/leaver workflows.
4. Open **Vendor Risk Review Queue** to explain SOC 2 report review for scope, complementary user entity controls, subservice organizations, exceptions, and actual service coverage.
5. Open **Evidence Worth Collecting** to distinguish useful operating proof from low-value documentation.
6. Show **BCDR and Incident Response Readiness** plus **Fraud and Funds-Transfer Controls** to demonstrate owner sign-off and finance partnership.
7. Close with **AI Tooling and Production AI Threat Model** and the safe boundary note.

## Short talk track

“This is synthetic, but it shows the operating model: define owners, automate the boring evidence, reduce friction, and focus on controls people will actually use. The dashboard favors evidence that helps operators make better decisions rather than screenshots or policy text with no operating proof.”

## What not to claim

Avoid presenting this demo as a compliance determination, certification result, legal conclusion, financial assurance artifact, or proof that a control operated effectively. It is a local readiness and review-prioritization demo using synthetic data.

## Mapping to small-company security program needs

- **Policy & privacy stack:** policy map, acknowledgements, review cadence, exception handling, and privacy counsel checkpoints.
- **Compliance evidence:** reusable evidence packets for customer asks, HIPAA-grade data controls, EU privacy obligations, California privacy obligations, and SOC 2-oriented readiness questions.
- **Vendor risk:** critical vendor queue with SOC 2 report review, contract safeguards, data sensitivity, and recommended reviewers.
- **Identity & access:** SSO, MFA, privileged access, joiner/mover/leaver workflows, SCIM, stale account detection, service accounts, and exceptions.
- **IT operations:** Apple MDM, endpoint security, asset lifecycle, office network ownership, conferencing, and AV ownership.
- **Operational security loop:** DLP/data leakage review, backups, access controls, risk assessments, and exception follow-up.
- **BCDR and incident response:** tested recovery, escalation paths, customer notification decision paths, tabletop exercises, and post-incident review.
- **Fraud and funds-transfer controls:** dual approval, callback verification, payment-change approval, suspicious request escalation, audit trail retention, and finance partnership.
- **AI tooling:** AI tool policy, AI vendor review, prompt/data leakage risk, provider retention review, production AI incident path, human review for sensitive outputs, and AI-assisted security tooling guardrails.

## Safe boundary note

This demo uses synthetic data to illustrate security-program readiness, control ownership, evidence workflows, and review prioritization. It does not determine compliance, audit readiness, legal adequacy, or control operating effectiveness.
